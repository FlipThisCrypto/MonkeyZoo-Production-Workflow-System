# Character Development Levels

Store one development level in every Character Bible. The level describes how much guidance exists, not how important the character is.

## Level 1: Visual Identity

The character may only have recognizable appearance, current series name, one signature visual feature, a basic role, and possibly a catchphrase.

Appropriate for background or newly discovered characters.

## Level 2: Personality Seed

The character has two or three dominant traits, one flaw, one strength, a basic speech style, and one recurring behavior.

Appropriate for supporting characters entering regular use.

## Level 3: Established Character

The character has clear motivations, relationships, emotional reactions, skills, weaknesses, running gags, and story functions.

Appropriate for recurring cast members.

## Level 4: Evolving Character

The character has personal history, internal conflicts, long-term goals, character arcs, changing relationships, and lessons learned from published issues.

Appropriate for characters carrying arcs across issues.

## Level 5: Core Canon Character

The character has strong immutable canon, detailed writing rules, established relationships, story continuity, multiple emotional layers, and restrictions preventing out-of-character writing.

Appropriate for core leads and heavily documented characters.

## Sparsity Rules

- Core lead characters may begin with 5 to 8 strong personality elements.
- Supporting characters may begin with 3 to 5.
- Minor characters may begin with 1 to 3.
- Background characters may begin with visual identity only.
- No character must have traits from all trait categories.
- A blank category is better than a weak or generic trait.
- Do not add a quirk simply because a field exists.
