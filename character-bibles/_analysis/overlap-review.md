# Overlap Review

## Similarity Conflicts

- Moodz and Emo remain the largest identity risk. Emo is inventoried as duplicate or variant, so no separate Emo Bible was created in this confirmed-character pass. Recommendation: decide whether Emo is a faction label, a Moodz variant, or a separate individual before writing any personal canon.
- Clever and TwoTone both solve problems analytically. Adjustment: Clever reads mechanisms and evidence; TwoTone reads balance and tradeoffs.
- Static and Cheeky can both produce high-energy comedy. Adjustment: Static comedy comes from overload and warning; Cheeky comedy comes from intentional misdirection and performance.
- Lil Devil and Cheeky can both escalate scenes. Adjustment: Lil Devil escalates through force; Cheeky escalates through attention and pranks.
- Super Monkey and NeonBlue can both be rescuers. Adjustment: Super performs visible saves; NeonBlue refuses abandonment and protects vulnerable people.
- Zombie and Scarline both carry mystery weight. Adjustment: Zombie recognizes old systems through lived/embodied traces; Scarline interprets patterns with grounded experience.

## No Immediate Overload

The initial Bibles keep supporting characters at Level 2 and Patch at Level 1 to avoid overbuilding.
