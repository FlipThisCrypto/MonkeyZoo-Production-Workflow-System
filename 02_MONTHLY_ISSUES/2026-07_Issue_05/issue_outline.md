# MZ-2026-07-05 — The Ones Who Stayed

Logline: Summoned back by a cheerful recall notice, the squad finds the
monkeys who never stopped upgrading — and Moodz learns that the second time
you face the machine, you don't have to scream.

Rejected titles: *Wellness Recall* (sells the joke, not the heart),
*MORE* (great poster, unsearchable).

Theme: Appetite can't be unplugged — but choice can be left open.
Page count: 8 story pages + 4 cover surfaces.

Emotional arc (Moodz): wary of the summons → back in the building that almost
erased him → calm, practiced abort → walks out taller. Panic has become
competence.
Emotional arc (NeonBlue, secondary): "free update!" optimism → meets Patch,
the friend who stayed → smile-drop (his once-per-issue beat) → chooses to
leave the door open rather than "fix" him.

Comedy arc: Static's spoken danger-level meter (4… of 4 → 4.5 → FIVE → "zero.
…one."), escalating against NeonBlue's collapsing ad-copy ("Breaks are
self-care."). The Stayed's marketing-moan chants ("MORE… utility…") do satire
and menace in the same breath.

Conflict: The squad wants answers about the recall; the building wants to
finish everyone's "update". The Stayed want nothing anymore — that's the horror.

Ending image/line: Patch stopped at the open threshold, rain outside.
Caption: "Choice is the upgrade." (Ed.4's closing thesis, paid forward.)

Teaser: Rooftop, red-masked silhouette, caption "Someone else got the recall
notice too." → Super faction issue.
