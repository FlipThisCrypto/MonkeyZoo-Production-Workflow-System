# MZ-2026-08-06 — The Broken Signal

Logline: Following a signal that should have died years ago, the squad finds
the monkeys the early fusion experiments left half-finished — and learns that
some of the people you failed don't want saving, just company.

Rejected titles: *Relay* (cold, no promise), *The Pending* (names the reveal
on the cover).

Theme: Walking away isn't abandoning — if you come back.
Page count: 8 story pages + 4 cover surfaces.

Emotional arc (Moodz): still carrying Issue 05's open door → certain the
signal is a trap → realizes the trap framing was self-protection ("if it's a
trap, leaving is smart; if it's people, leaving is what we did last time") →
chooses to witness instead of fix → makes the squad's first return-promise.
Emotional arc (Patch, secondary): recognizes the signal, can't/won't explain →
his silence breaks not with words but with an offering: he leaves his recall
notice — the paper that brought him back — with the people still waiting.

Comedy arc: Static's danger meter goes qualitative under stress ("Danger
level: someone's been HERE."); Ash's four-word audits of ancient
infrastructure ("Gifts knock." energy, now aimed at cable management); the
Pending's bureaucratic patience played absurdly straight ("Is it done yet?" —
asked the way you ask about microwave popcorn, after 13 years).

Conflict: NeonBlue wants to rescue, Moodz suspects a trap, Patch knows and
won't say — and the Pending want none of the above. Want vs obstacle: the
squad wants to DO something; the only right thing to do is almost nothing.

Ending image/line: Empty relay room, console pulse shifts rhythm, screen:
"SIGNAL ACKNOWLEDGED — RETURN PROTOCOL INITIATED." Caption: "It was never
calling for help. It was calling something back."

Teaser: rear cover — the same wrong-rhythm red blink, answered by a second
light far beyond the city limits. Copy: "NEXT: IT HEARD."
